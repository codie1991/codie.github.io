How I work

Good teams don't happen by accident.

The most important thing I can do as a manager is create the conditions for smart people to do their best work. That means being direct, being clear about what good looks like, and making it genuinely safe to raise problems early.

Understand deeply

I stay close enough to understand what's actually happening. The real constraints, the blockers, the trade-offs. That's how I make informed decisions and give teams the context to work autonomously

Outcomes over output

We measure impact through data. Which features move metrics that matter? What's actually working? I build teams that use evidence (not opinions) to guide what we do next.

Safe to fail, fast to learn

I don't treat psychological safety as a perk, it's how teams do their best work. When people can tell the truth without fear of blame, everything improves: iteration speed, retention, real growth.

Enable growth, keep talent

I invest in clear pathways, honest feedback, and creating the conditions for people to learn, level up, and lead. Not just in reviews, but in how we work together every day.
