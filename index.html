How I work

Good teams don't happen by accident.

The most important thing I can do as a manager is create the conditions for smart people to do their best work. That means being direct, being clear about what good looks like, and making it genuinely safe to raise problems early.

Stay close to the work

Not in the weeds, but close enough to ask the right questions, spot hidden risk before it becomes a crisis, and earn real credibility with the engineers I lead.

Outcomes over output

Shipping is great. Shipping something that moves a metric that matters (and understanding why) is better. I try to build teams that actually care about the difference.

Psychological safety isn't soft

Teams that can tell each other the truth surface problems faster, iterate faster, and retain better people. I invest in it deliberately, not as a nice-to-have.

People who grow, stay

I care a lot about individual growth. Structured coaching, honest feedback, clear pathways. Not just in review cycles, but as an ongoing conversation.
